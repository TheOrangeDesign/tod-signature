Email Signature Image Generation Guide

Purpose
This file explains how to generate the signature portrait image using Gemini.
Use this when creating or updating email signature visuals for TheOrange.Design or client use.

Image Goal
You are creating a hyper realistic portrait used for professional signature blocks.
The image must feel premium, clean, and studio shot.
Target output size is 1080 x 1920 vertical.

Where This Will Be Used
Email signatures
Team profile cards
Internal brand assets
Client facing communications

Generation Tool
Google Gemini image generation

How To Use
Step 1. Open Gemini image generation.
Step 2. Paste the full prompt below.
Step 3. Set output resolution to 1080 x 1920 if available.
Step 4. Generate 3 to 5 versions.
Step 5. Select the most natural looking skin texture and lighting balance.

Gemini Prompt
Create a hyperrealistic vertical portrait shot in 1080x1920 format, characterized by stark cinematic lighting and intense contrast. The composition evokes quiet dominance and sculptural elegance. The background shifts into a gradient of saturated tones ranging from #FEAD1A to #FF6804, but the lighting on the subject remains clean and controlled, with no color bleed from the backdrop. The subject wears a black hoodie with the hood down, its heavy fabric framing the shoulders and chest while keeping the face fully visible. Lighting is tightly directional and extreme, flooding one side of the face with warm neutral golden highlights while plunging the other side into near-total darkness, so dramatic that half the face is barely visible. This sharp split-light effect sculpts the bone structure with architectural precision while creating a sense of mystery and quiet defiance. The subject’s expression is unreadable and cool-toned eyes half-lidded, lips relaxed suggesting detachment or restrained intensity. The hoodie’s matte texture contrasts with the skin’s smooth, dewy glow, adding depth without distraction. Minimal retouching preserves natural skin texture and slight imperfections, amplifying realism. Editorial tension is built through close cropping, tonal control, and the oppressive intimacy of the camera’s proximity. There are no props or accessories the visual impact is created purely through light, shadow, color saturation, and posture evoking high fashion, isolation, and hyper-modern masculinity.
